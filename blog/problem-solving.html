Problem Solving

A time I was blocked on a simple problem.

I found my self stuck when we were introduced to loops. There was so much new syntax and jargon, it felt quite overwhelming and scrambled what I had learnt about JS. I ended up going right back to the start of our JS intro videos and re-watching everything, researched terms with google and had chat gpt break everything down to the very simplest versions of things.
It all felt quite overwhelming at the time, but breaking down and simplifying everything really helped me get a small grasp on loop fundamentals. I learnt about a few handy resources for developers, like MDN Web Docs and how I can use chat gpt to help me break down code and explain each step and part that I am having trouble with.

A time I elegantly solved a problem.

I was pleased to find the .map method, when stuck on the superflipflop exercise and wanted to apply a function to objects in an array. I did some research through google, MDN etc until I found the .map method, which worked perfectly for my problem at hand. Initially I felt a bit overwhelmed by the exercise and then took a step back, wrote out some pseudocode in my notebook, did some research and re-tackled the problem. I learnt that stepping back, research and pseudocode are all very handy things to use when stuck.
