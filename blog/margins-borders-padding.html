The difference between Margin, Border, and Padding when using CSS

If you are new to CSS then learning about margins, borders and padding, and the difference between them, is crucial to good web design. You can use the image below as a good reference to help understand the following points.

It all starts with our content, this is the information we want displayed, for example an image or some text. We add that into the html document and then it is displayed very simply on our page and usually will need some CSS magic to help it fit in right and look good.

The border is then the framing around our content, like a picture frame around a painting. It can be altered, for colours, styles and many other design options, as well as being left out all together, if you do not want/require one for your content. You can see I have added a border to this paragraph (our content), however it is hugging our content very closely, that's the job of padding, we'll deal with that next.

The padding is our space between the content and the border. It can be a uniform amount of distance around the content or you can set different values for the top, sides and bottom. You can see now that the border is further away from our content as I have added 20px of padding to out element.

So we have our content nested inside our border which is spaced out with padding and finally the margin, which is the space between our border and other elements or the edge of a screen. You can see I have altered this elements margins to be further away from the other elements and the edge of the screen.

I hope you found this informative and can use what you have learnt on your own coding journey!
