AFTER FOUNDATIONS

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goals are much the same as when I started foundations. The ability to work remotely from anywhere and have flexible hours.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Working on a computer has been quite enjoyable and I’ve really enjoyed pushing my brain with new problems and information. I have found some parts quite challenging but have given them appropriate time and breaks. I’ll take this into account when I encounter problems in bootcamp.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

The main challenges I think I will face at bootcamp are keeping up with/on top of the material and keeping motivated.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’ve not worked much within groups and I am not a great public speaker, so it would be nice to develop some skills around those.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Support when I need it and to put up with a million questions from me when I am stuck or confused!

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

Work/study hard and to make the most of my time and resources at Dev Academy.

BEFORE FOUNDATIONS

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I would like to be able to work remotely from around the world and have flexible hours.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

Having not worked at a computer before and coming from the background of a motorcycle mechanic, I think that sitting all day in front of a computer could prove to be a limitation but we shall see! I enjoy problem solving and learning new things.

3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Self motivation, as I’ve not worked/studied remotely before, this will be new for me.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

4. I’ll put aside time each day to study and take breaks, and be kind to others that I work with. If I find myself becoming stressed or the workload impacting me, I will take time away from the screen to refocus, identify and minimise non-study stressors, and get back into it.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

If I become stuck, after trying to resolve the issue myself, through wireframing on paper or sitting back and looking at the problem from a different angle, I will ask for help from my classmates and facilitators.
